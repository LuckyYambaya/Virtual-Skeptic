About Virtual Skeptic

A corner of the internet for the unfiltered realities of working virtually.

The origin

Why I started this blog

I've been working virtually for years. Through different roles, different clients, different time zones, and different versions of what "remote work" means depending on who's defining it.

What I kept noticing — and what kept bothering me — was the gap between how virtual work is packaged online and what it actually feels like to live it. The glossy productivity content. The hustle-flavored advice. The threads that make it look like everyone with a laptop and a Wi-Fi connection is thriving.

Virtual Skeptic is my attempt to talk about it honestly. The good stuff, the frustrating stuff, and the stuff in between that doesn't fit neatly into a listicle.

"Skeptic doesn't mean cynical. It means I'd rather think it through than take it on faith."

What you'll find here

The kind of writing I do

This blog covers the world of virtual and remote work — but through the lens of someone actually doing it, not consulting about it. You'll find:

💬

Opinions

Takes on remote work trends, industry news, and the ideas everyone seems to accept without questioning.

🛠

Practical Advice

Tools, habits, and frameworks that actually held up after real use — not just things that sound good in theory.

🔍

Perspectives

Different angles on the remote work experience — productivity, isolation, culture, money, time, identity.

📰

Current Events

Reactions to relevant news — RTO mandates, AI tools, platform changes, and what they mean for virtual workers.

A note on trust

No hidden agenda

I don't accept sponsored posts. If I recommend a tool, it's because I use it. If I criticise something, it's because I genuinely think there's a problem worth naming. When this blog eventually runs ads, they'll be clearly labelled and won't influence what I write.

That's the whole point of the "Skeptic" part. I'm not here to sell you anything except, hopefully, a useful perspective.
